================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. OVERALL NYC ENTROPY (48.9%):
   - Indicates low-medium uncertainty in food access
   - System is relatively organized
   - 155 out of 190 zip codes (81.6%) have abundant access

2. BOROUGH VARIATION:
   - Highest entropy: Manhattan (1.357 bits)
     → Most internal variation/unpredictability
   - Lowest entropy: Brooklyn (0.176 bits)
     → Most uniform/predictable

3. KL DIVERGENCE (Outlier Detection):
   - Most different from NYC average: Brooklyn (KL = 0.222)
   - Most similar to NYC average: Queens (KL = 0.052)

4. FOOD DESERTS:
   - Total: 14 zip codes (7.4%)
   - Concentrated in specific boroughs (see detailed statistics)

================================================================================
WHAT THESE NUMBERS MEAN
================================================================================

Shannon Entropy (H = 0.978 bits, 48.9%):
→ At 48.9% of maximum, NYC food access is more predictable than random
→ If you pick a random zip code, you can guess "Abundant" with 81.6% accuracy
→ This low-medium entropy suggests systematic patterns exist

KL Divergence:
→ Shows which boroughs have DIFFERENT food access patterns
→ High KL = Need borough-specific policies
→ Low KL = Similar to city average, general policies work

================================================================================
POLICY IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

1. Target the 14 food desert zip codes for intervention
2. Brooklyn requires different approach (highest KL divergence)
3. Manhattan has highest internal variation (H = 1.357)
   - May need multiple sub-borough strategies
4. Brooklyn is most uniform (H = 0.176)
   - One-size-fits-all policy likely effective
